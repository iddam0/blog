Let's Have a Talk About Pronouns

Why learning pronouns is important., March 3, 2019

My name is Maddi. I identify as trans and non-binary, and I go by they/them pronouns. I'm here to have a conversation about why it is necessary to learn how to properly use pronouns. There is a multitude of pronouns and neopronouns, and while it may see overwhelming if you've never heard of them, I implore you to read this post so you can understand. Every single person has pronouns..

I'm sure you've heard the basic pronouns, he/him, she/her, and they/them; but have you heard of neopronouns? Don't be overwhelmed - you use these pronouns exactly how you would use the more 'popular' ones. For example, the neopronouns ze/zir can be used in a sentence like this: "I like zir outfit. Ze wears it very well!" It isn't difficult, and it is important that you refer to people with their proper pronouns. Personally, it really irks me when people call me gendered words, such as "sis" or "ma'am," because I identify as non-binary, meaning I do not identify as the gender I was assigned at birth, and really don't identify with male/female at all. I just look at my gender like I have none, if that makes sense. I like gender-neutral terms, and always get a smile on my face when people refer to me as my proper pronouns. It is harmful for trans people to hear their loved ones misgender them; I would know, because all of my family still calls me she/her.
